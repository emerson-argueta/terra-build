Executive Summary

California's housing crisis is structural, not cyclical. The construction industry — protected by lobbying, favorable building codes, and institutional inertia — has maintained pricing that puts homeownership out of reach for most Californians. Terra Build exists to break that system through cost, not politics.

Adobe — compressed earth construction — is one of the oldest and most thermally efficient building materials on earth. It is also dramatically cheaper than conventional construction when properly engineered, manufactured, and deployed. The barriers to its use in California are not physical or scientific. They are regulatory, institutional, and political.

The goal is not profit. It is to make the current pricing model of conventional California construction economically unsustainable — forcing the industry to adapt or exit.

Terra Build removes those barriers through three integrated systems:

  • Manufacturing. A mobile unit produces certified, seismically-engineered adobe blocks on any job site using local soil — cutting structural wall costs to $12,000–$20,000 per house.
  • Software. A platform that translates existing seismic science into location-specific geometric building designs — making every structure optimized for its exact site conditions.
  • Policy. A 10-year strategy to achieve California code recognition — using research, affordable housing coalitions, and the political cost of blocking a cheaper construction method.
02

The Problem

California's Housing Crisis Is a Construction Cost Crisis

California has a housing shortfall of approximately 3.5 million units. The primary driver is not land scarcity — it is construction cost. A standard single-family home in California costs $300–$500 per square foot to build, before land. That makes homeownership mathematically impossible for most California households.

How the industry maintains this

  • Building codes written by committees dominated by conventional material interests
  • Insurance frameworks that refuse to underwrite alternative methods, blocking financing
  • Contractor licensing structures that discourage alternative material expertise
  • No funded research pathway for alternative materials to achieve code recognition

The primary barriers to adobe in California are regulatory and political — not physical or scientific. The engineering solutions to seismic resistance exist. The manufacturing technology exists. What has been missing is the integrated system that makes it buildable, certifiable, and affordable at scale.

03

Engineering Foundation

Seismic Performance

The conventional narrative is that adobe cannot withstand California earthquakes. This is a half-truth. Unreinforced traditional adobe performs poorly in major seismic events. Engineered adobe — optimized for specific site conditions — performs significantly better. Key findings from shake table testing at PUCP (Peru), UC Berkeley, and EERI field studies:

  • External polymer or basalt fiber mesh arrests crack propagation — walls crack but stay standing
  • Wall geometry is as important as material — height-to-thickness ratios and buttress spacing dramatically affect performance
  • Latex-modified clay mortar at joints distributes energy absorption throughout the entire wall
  • Combined interventions have survived equivalent M7.5 ground motion in controlled testing

The Geometry-Seismicity Translation — Core Innovation

Every location has a unique seismic signature — not just magnitude, but frequency content, pulse duration, fault directionality, and soil amplification. These characteristics are precisely mapped by USGS and the California Geological Survey. What has never been done is translating that data into geometric building rules for adobe construction. Terra Build's software performs this translation.

The Engineered Wall System

1. Functionally Graded Wall Composition

The dense thermal core — approximately 60% of wall thickness — is pure high-density adobe providing maximum thermal mass. The outer 40% is basalt-fiber-rich adobe providing ductility and crack arrest. Thermal mass is fully preserved where it matters most.

2. Latex-Modified Clay Mortar

Natural rubber latex added to clay mortar creates a viscoelastic joint that absorbs and converts seismic energy to heat. Under rapid earthquake loading, joints stiffen and dissipate energy. Under slow thermal cycling, they behave normally.

3. External Basalt Fiber Mesh

Applied to the exterior face after construction. Basalt fiber is derived from volcanic rock — thermally and chemically compatible with earthen construction, with higher tensile strength than fiberglass and dramatically lower thermal conductivity than steel.

4. Low-Tech Base Isolation

Recycled rubber pads and rounded river stones in a contained sand layer between foundation and wall base reduce transmitted ground acceleration by an estimated 20–40% for moderate seismic events. Simple, cheap, and effective as one layer in a multi-layer system.

Manufacturing System

The Mobile Production Unit (MPU)

A single trailer-mounted unit that brings industrial block manufacturing to any job site, eliminating factory overhead and transportation cost for the heaviest material — the soil itself.

The fundamental cost advantage: soil is free or near-free on most California construction sites. By producing blocks on-site, the MPU eliminates the cost of transporting tons of raw material — the largest hidden cost in conventional masonry construction.

Station Configuration

Jaw crusher and screen processes raw excavated soil. Removes debris, sizes particles. 2–3 tons per hour.

Industrial pan mixer or pugmill meters all components precisely. Consistent mix regardless of operator skill level.

Hydraulic CEB press produces interlocking certified blocks at 300–500 per hour. Each block QR-coded with full production record.

05

Lightweight shade structure with forced air circulation. Moisture monitoring certifies completion. 3–5 days vs weeks for sun drying.

The Amendment Package System

Pre-formulated, pre-measured additive sets that correct variable local soil to a consistent certified specification — similar to how concrete admixtures work but designed for earthen construction. Each package contains basalt fiber, natural latex concentrate, clay mineral correctors, stabilizer, and moisture retention polymer.

This is the razor-blade in the business model — every MPU in the field consumes amendment packages continuously. Packages are lightweight, high-value, and easy to ship regionally. Priced at cost for the mission, not for margin.

05

Software Platform

A six-layer system that translates publicly available seismic science into certified, buildable adobe designs for any California location.

Layer 1 — Site Seismic Analysis Engine

Integrates USGS Unified Hazard Tool API, fault databases, VS30 soil maps, and CGS microzonation data. Computes peak ground acceleration, full site-specific response spectrum, dominant period, near-fault classification, and soil amplification factors for any GPS coordinate.

Layer 2 — The Translation Engine (Core Innovation)

Converts the seismic signature into geometric design parameters. Sub-components include the wall proportion calculator, buttress geometry engine, corner optimization module, opening constraint calculator, plan shape advisor, and foundation/isolation calculator. This translation layer has never been built before.

Layer 3 — Archetype Matching and Generative Design

Prescriptive mode: a library of pre-certified geometric archetypes organized by seismic zone — builders select and build without custom engineering. Parametric mode: topology-optimized custom design for architects, generating compliant options ranked by predicted seismic performance.

Layer 4 — Block Specification Integration

Connects geometric design to the manufactured block system. Returns block specs, amendment package requirements, material quantities, and certified product references. One workflow from site address to procurement list.

Layer 5 — Output and Documentation Engine

Produces construction-ready drawings, structural specifications, material schedules, performance reports, code compliance packages for variance applications, and inspector checklists for each wall assembly.

Layer 6 — Validation and Feedback Loop

Post-earthquake performance data from instrumented buildings refines translation rules over time. Every seismic event improves predictive accuracy. Machine learning refinement as real-world data accumulates.

Political & Regulatory Strategy

Building codes are a lagging indicator — they follow political and economic reality, they don't lead it. Win the political conditions and the code follows. Fight the code directly and you spend a decade in committee rooms while nothing gets built.

Phase 1 — Build Evidence Nobody Can Argue With

  • UC Berkeley PEER Partnership. Most credible seismic institution in California. Frame as housing affordability research to attract HCD funding.
  • PUCP Partnership. Peru's leading adobe earthquake testing lab. Brings decades of existing data into the California technical record immediately.
  • Certification Database. Every MPU-produced block feeds the database. After 100 projects, Terra Build holds a performance dataset no conventional material can match for transparency.

Phase 2 — Geographic Entry Strategy

  • Rural First. Unincorporated counties — Nevada, Tuolumne, Trinity — have simpler permitting and building departments willing to engage directly.
  • Owner-Builder Exemption. California Health and Safety Code Section 19482 creates a legal pathway for variance applications with politically sympathetic optics.
  • New Mexico Precedent. Their 1991 adobe code is the existence proof. Their code language is the template.

Phase 3 — The Affordable Housing Political Vehicle

The political frame: California is blocking a construction method that cuts housing costs by 60–70%. That story belongs to YIMBY Action, Habitat for Humanity California, California Community Land Trust Network, and environmental groups. The specific legislative vehicle is a certified adobe ADU pilot program — lower stakes, politically popular, exactly how cob construction entered the IRC.

Phase 4 — Insurance Bridge

  • Surplus Lines First. Operate outside standard market frameworks. The certification database makes this conversation possible.
  • Lloyd's Engagement. A Lloyd's binder covering certified Terra Build construction would give conventional insurers cover to follow.
  • Actuarial Data Build. 5–7 years of insured project data supports ISO classification — which opens standard insurance and conventional financing.

Phase 5 — Formal Code Engagement

Only after phases 1–4 have built the foundation. Target an IRC earthen construction appendix covering CEB and manufactured adobe. Separately target a California-specific HCD standard framed as a housing affordability initiative. Sustained presence on ICC technical committees is non-negotiable — this is how the cob advocates won.

Organizational Model

To execute the mission — make adobe so affordable it forces the industry to adapt — the organizational structure must prevent the cost inflation that investor return requirements create.

SoftwareOpen-source core, free to owner-builders. Low-cost commercial license. Funded by certification fees — not by inflating costs for those who need it most.
MPU FleetOwned by regional cooperatives. Member builders pay cost-based rental. Surplus funds next MPU purchase. Same model as agricultural equipment cooperatives — proven at scale for a century.
Amendment PackagesSold at cost plus minimal operating margin. A fraction of equivalent conventional material costs.
CertificationSmall per-block fee funds database and ongoing research. Creates the data asset that drives code recognition and insurance underwriting.
ResearchNonprofit partnership with UC system and PUCP. Funded by HCD housing research grants, foundations, and a portion of certification revenues.

The cooperative structure means the mission is structurally protected. It cannot be acquired and repriced by a conventional industry player. No investor can demand returns that inflate prices to the builders we are trying to enable.

Open Infrastructure & Licensing

Terra Build's legal architecture is as deliberate as its engineering architecture. Every component is licensed to maximize adoption, prevent predatory capture, and ensure improvements flow back to the commons. The goal is a system that cannot be acquired, closed, or repriced by any private interest — not through goodwill, but through law.

Open licensing is not altruism here. It is the mechanism that makes the cooperative model structurally durable. A proprietary system can be bought. An open system with the right licenses cannot be closed — only forked, and forks must remain open.

The Finalized License Map

ComponentLicenseRole in the Ecosystem
TerraEngine Software AGPL-3.0 Closes the SaaS loophole. Any firm that hosts the translation engine as a web service must publish their source code. The seismic-to-geometry logic cannot be captured and sold without giving back.
Amendment Package Formulations CC BY-SA 4.0 The Open Razor Blade. Chemical specifications are free to use and manufacture. Any improvement to a formulation must be shared under the same terms. Prevents predatory patenting of the core consumable.
MPU Manufacturing Specs CC BY-SA 4.0 Any cooperative can build their own Mobile Production Unit from these specifications. Hardware improvements discovered in the field must be shared back. The fleet improves collectively.
Performance Database CC0 — Public Domain Zero legal friction for insurers, researchers, and code bodies. No attribution requirement, no license negotiation. The earthquake performance data exists to be used. Frictionlessness is the point.
Policy & Strategy Documents CC BY-SA 4.0 Local YIMBY groups and housing advocates can adapt the variance application templates and political roadmap for their specific county building departments. Adaptations must credit Terra Build and share under the same terms — creating a traceable lineage of policy development.
Archetype Designs CC BY 4.0 Architects can use certified archetypes in commercial projects without ShareAlike infecting their broader design work. Maximum adoption is the goal. Every commercial project built from a Terra Build archetype is a performance data point and a public demonstration.

Why Each License Was Chosen

AGPL-3.0 over GPLv3 for Software

TerraEngine is a web application. A company could run GPLv3 software as a hosted service, charge for access, and never distribute the binary — technically complying with GPLv3 while capturing all the value. AGPL closes that by treating network use as distribution. Any firm that hosts a modified TerraEngine must publish their source code.

CC BY-SA over CC BY-ND for Policy Documents

NoDerivatives would prevent a Sacramento housing advocacy group from adapting the variance application template for Sacramento County's specific building department language. That adaptation is exactly what the strategy needs to happen. ShareAlike allows adaptation while maintaining attribution and requiring derivatives to remain open — creating a spreading network of policy tools rather than a locked document.

CC BY over CC BY-SA for Archetypes

An architect doing a commercial project will not use an archetype if ShareAlike might be interpreted as infecting their broader design work. CC BY removes that friction entirely. The architect can adapt the archetype, charge for their services, and the only obligation is crediting Terra Build. Adoption matters more than control here.

CC0 for Performance Data

The data's value comes entirely from being used — by actuaries modeling insurance risk, by researchers publishing peer-reviewed studies, by code bodies evaluating technical evidence. Any license, even a permissive one requiring attribution, creates friction that slows those uses. CC0 eliminates all friction. The certification system that generates the data is AGPL-protected. The data itself belongs to everyone.

The Trademark Layer

Licenses govern what others can do with Terra Build's designs and code. A registered trademark on Terra Build Certified governs what others can call their products. Someone can legally take the CC BY-SA licensed MPU design, build their own unit, and produce blocks with it. What they cannot do is call those blocks Terra Build Certified without going through the certification system. The trademark protects the quality assurance chain even in a fully open ecosystem. Register it early — before the system has enough visibility to attract imitators.
