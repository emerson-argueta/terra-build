Terra Build is intentionally open. The licenses below are not suggestions — they are binding legal conditions that govern how any part of this system may be implemented, manufactured, or deployed.
!

If you are building, manufacturing, hosting, or commercializing anything derived from Terra Build intellectual property — you are subject to the terms below. The open licenses grant broad rights. Those rights come with mandatory obligations. Ignoring the obligations does not dissolve them; it creates legal liability. Read carefully before you build.

License Summary
What's Licensed and How

Every component of Terra Build carries a specific license chosen to maximize adoption while preventing predatory closure. The table below is a summary — the full license texts govern in all cases.

ComponentLicenseEnforcement Logic
TerraEngine Software Seismic translation engine, design generation, site analysis tools
AGPL-3.0
The Affero GPL closes the SaaS loophole. Any entity running TerraEngine as a network service — even internally — must publish complete corresponding source code to users of that service. Hosting this software and keeping your modifications private is not permitted.
Amendment Formulations Basalt fiber ratios, latex modifier specs, mineral correction protocols
CC BY-SA 4.0
You may manufacture and sell amendment packages freely. Any derivative formulation — any modification to the blend — must be published under CC BY-SA 4.0. You cannot patent, trade-secret, or otherwise encumber a derivative formulation and remove it from the commons.
MPU Manufacturing Specifications Mechanical design, compression tolerances, QR certification system
CC BY-SA 4.0
Any cooperative or manufacturer can build a Mobile Production Unit. Improvements discovered in manufacture or operation must be documented and published under the same license. Field-discovered optimizations cannot be withheld from the network.
Performance Database Block test results, seismic performance records, thermal data
CC0
No restrictions. No attribution required. Insurers, code bodies, researchers, and regulators may use this data freely in any form. Frictionlessness is the point — the data's value is in being used, not controlled.
Policy & Strategy Documents Code petition templates, advocacy frameworks, coalition guides
CC BY-SA 4.0
Local groups may adapt building code petitions and advocacy materials for their county or jurisdiction. Adaptations must credit Terra Build and be released under CC BY-SA 4.0. Policy forks must remain open — a traceable, usable lineage.
Archetype Designs Certified floor plans, structural geometry templates, seismic archetypes
CC BY 4.0
Architects and developers may use certified archetypes in commercial projects without ShareAlike requirements applying to their broader design work. Attribution to Terra Build required. Every project using an archetype generates a performance data point — adoption is the goal.

Trademark Policy
What Open Licenses
Don't Cover

The open licenses govern what you can do with Terra Build designs, software, and specifications. The Terra Build Certified trademark sits above those licenses and governs what you can call your products. Open licenses and trademark protection serve different, complementary purposes.

What the Trademark Protects

Quality and Accountability

The Terra Build Certified mark guarantees that a block, MPU, or design meets verified performance standards. Anyone can build with the open specs. Only authorized implementations that pass certification testing can carry the mark. This protects the public — not Terra Build's commercial interests.

Permitted Trademark Uses

Referring to Terra Build Accurately

You may accurately state that your product is "compatible with Terra Build specifications," "derived from Terra Build open-source designs," or "built under AGPL-3.0 licensed TerraEngine." You may not use the Terra Build Certified mark or the Terra Build wordmark in product names without authorization.

How to Get Certified

Certification Process

Certification requires documented block performance data submitted to the public database, third-party structural review, and compliance with the current Terra Build production standards. The process is designed to be accessible to cooperatives — not just large manufacturers. Contact licensing@terrabuild.org to begin.

Why This Matters

Protecting the Mission

A system that can be imitated without accountability can be undermined by poor-quality imitations. The trademark ensures that "Terra Build Certified" blocks will perform. It makes the open system durable — cheap imitations don't carry the mark, and the mark means something because it can't be freely taken.

The open licenses prevent capture by incumbents. The trademark prevents degradation by imitators. Both protections serve the same goal: ensuring that the system that makes housing affordable remains affordable and functional indefinitely.
