INTRO

The Question This Document Answers

Most cost comparisons for alternative construction methods look only at the component that favors them most. This analysis does the opposite: it goes through every major cost component honestly — including the ones where Terra Build does not outperform conventional construction — and produces three realistic total-cost scenarios for a 1,500 square foot single-story California home.

The three scenarios reflect different phases of Terra Build's development: today (without code recognition), at full system maturity (with code recognition), and the owner-builder case (which eliminates contractor labor for wall construction).

Land cost is excluded from all scenarios. Terra Build addresses construction costs. California land prices are a separate crisis this system does not directly solve — though dramatically lower construction costs change the math on what land price is affordable.

04

Honest Caveats and Limitations

Every number in this analysis depends on conditions that are not guaranteed. Here are the key variables that could push outcomes in either direction.

⚠ Land Cost Not Addressed
Terra Build creates significant construction cost savings but does nothing about California land prices, which can exceed construction cost in many markets. The savings change the affordability math — but land remains a separate and significant constraint.
⚠ Labor Market Development
The savings compound as trained adobe labor becomes available at competitive rates. Early projects will face an unfamiliarity premium of 20–40% on wall construction labor. This improves at scale but cannot be assumed away in near-term projections.
⚠ Code Recognition Timeline
The engineering premium — $15,000–$50,000 per project — disappears only when the political strategy succeeds. Scenario B and C savings are contingent on achieving code recognition, which is an 8–10 year target, not a current reality.
⚠ Site-Specific Soil Variability
Not all California soils are suitable for the amendment system. Sites with extreme soil conditions — expansive clays, contaminated fill, certain coastal soils — may require imported soil at additional cost, reducing the structural wall savings from the baseline.
⚠ Scale Dependency
Amendment package costs and MPU cooperative rental rates improve as the cooperative network grows. Early projects will see slightly higher per-project costs than the mature-system estimates shown here.
⚠ Financing Access
Without code recognition, conventional mortgage financing is unavailable for adobe construction. Owner-builders may need to self-finance construction, which is challenging. This is partly why the policy strategy is inseparable from the cost story.
05

The Bottom Line

The construction cost savings are real and substantial across all three scenarios. But they operate within a California housing market where land, financing, and regulatory costs add layers that adobe alone cannot dissolve.

Scenario Conventional Total Terra Build Total
Today — pre-scale, no code recognition $388,000 $243,000  (−37%)
Full Scale — code recognized, trained labor $388,000 $190,000  (−51%)
Owner-Builder — self-supplied wall labor $450,000+ $190,000  (−58%+)

At Scenario C, a California family could build a 1,500 sq ft home for approximately $190,000 in construction costs. Adding $200,000–$400,000 for land depending on location, total all-in cost becomes $390,000–$590,000 — versus $700,000–$1,100,000+ for equivalent conventionally-built housing. That is not a marginal improvement. It is a different tier of housing accessibility entirely.
